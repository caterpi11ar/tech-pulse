# An AI Coding Skeptic Tries AI Coding — Here's What He Found

Simon Willison is one of the most credible independent voices in tech. When he changes his mind, it's worth paying attention.

## What Happened

Simon Willison — creator of Datasette, former Django core developer, and prolific tech blogger — published a detailed account of his deep dive into AI agent-assisted coding. Subtitled "in excessive detail," the post lives up to its name: every friction point, every AI mistake, and every moment where his skepticism shifted is documented. The post scored 10 points on Hacker News and spread widely across the developer community.

## Why It Matters

Willison isn't an AI evangelist, and he isn't an AI doomer. He's the kind of person who actually uses the tools, keeps notes, and writes honestly about what he finds. That's rare.

His findings (no spoilers) offer something benchmarks and product blogs can't: a ground-level account of what AI coding assistance actually feels like over an extended real project. What helps, what wastes your time, and why "AI makes writing code easier" and "AI makes being an engineer harder" can both be true at the same time.

## My Take

This piece matters because it represents a dying genre: careful skepticism, careful experimentation, careful documentation.

The discourse around AI coding has collapsed into two camps — "AI will replace all programmers" and "AI-generated code is garbage." Willison's post refuses both extremes and fills the messy middle with actual evidence. That's more useful than any product demo.

If you're a developer trying to figure out how to integrate these tools into your workflow, this is the most valuable thing you'll read this week.
