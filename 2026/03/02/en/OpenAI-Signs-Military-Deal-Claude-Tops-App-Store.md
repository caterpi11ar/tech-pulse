# OpenAI Signs Military Deal — Claude Tops the App Store Overnight

OpenAI handed its biggest competitor the best gift possible: a moral crisis.

## What Happened

In late February 2026, OpenAI signed a deal with the U.S. Department of Defense, enabling its AI to be used for military applications. The backlash was immediate and fierce. Reddit's r/ChatGPT flooded with account deletion screenshots. The "Cancel ChatGPT" movement went mainstream, with posts racking up thousands of upvotes and users compiling receipts of every promise Sam Altman broke.

Meanwhile, Anthropic publicly refused to surveil American citizens — a pointed contrast that landed perfectly. Within 15 hours, Claude climbed from #4 to #1 in the App Store. Even Katy Perry switched over.

## Why It Matters

This isn't just a PR stumble. It's a values fork in the AI industry.

OpenAI was founded on the premise of building AI "for the benefit of all humanity." That framing is now a liability. When you sign with the Pentagon, you don't get to keep the idealist origin story. And in a world where frontier models are increasingly capable and interchangeable, trust has become a genuine differentiator.

Anthropic played this with precision. Their message — "we won't help governments spy on you" — isn't just ethics. It's product positioning.

## My Take

Let's be honest: Anthropic isn't morally pure either. Its investors include government-adjacent capital, and today's principled stance could evaporate under a different contract offer. But in the court of public opinion, timing and optics are everything — and Anthropic won this round cleanly.

The deeper issue: the fact that "we don't help surveil you" needs to be *stated* as a selling point tells you exactly how blurry the line between AI labs and state power has become.

## What to Watch

- Will OpenAI's subscriber churn show up in their next earnings?
- Can Anthropic convert this attention spike into lasting retention?
- Which AI labs will be next to sign government deals — and will anyone notice?
