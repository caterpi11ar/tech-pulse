# Claude's Memory Import Feature: The Most Perfectly Timed Product Launch of 2026

Timing isn't everything. But sometimes it's the whole thing.

![Claude import](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6889473510b50328dbb70ae6/68c469d1859e3e7ecf6c4c95_og-default.png)

## What Happened

Anthropic quietly launched a feature allowing users to import their chat history from other AI platforms — including ChatGPT — directly into Claude, preserving context and memory. The feature is live at claude.com/import-memory.

This dropped at the peak of the "Cancel ChatGPT" movement, when hundreds of thousands of users were actively looking for a reason to switch. The post hit 17 points on Hacker News — one of the highest scores of the week.

## Why It Matters

The single biggest friction point in switching AI tools isn't capability — it's context. You've spent months building a relationship with an AI: your preferences, your projects, your writing style. Switching means starting over. That's a real cost that keeps people loyal to a platform even when they're unhappy with it.

This feature eliminates that cost. You can bring your memory with you.

From a product strategy perspective, this is textbook "competitor crisis window" execution: reduce migration cost precisely when your competitor has maximally damaged user trust.

## My Take

The timing is too perfect to be coincidental. Anthropic was watching OpenAI's PR disaster unfold and launched this at exactly the right moment.

But there's a deeper irony worth noting. This feature treats chat history as portable data — a user right. That's genuinely good for users today. But as Claude accumulates more of your memory, the same logic applies in reverse: eventually, leaving Claude will be just as costly as leaving ChatGPT was today.

The walled garden doesn't disappear. It just resets the clock.

## What to Watch

- Will other platforms build reciprocal import features?
- Could "AI chat portability" become a regulatory requirement, like GDPR's data portability rights?
- How much useful context does the import actually preserve in practice?
