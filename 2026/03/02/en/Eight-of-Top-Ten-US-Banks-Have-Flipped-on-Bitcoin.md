# Eight of the Top Ten US Banks Have Flipped on Bitcoin in Six Months

The same institutions that called Bitcoin a fraud are now lining up for custody licenses.

## What Happened

According to aggregated data shared on r/Bitcoin, 8 of the top 10 U.S. banks have reversed their official stance on Bitcoin over the past six months — moving from opposition or neutrality toward active engagement, including custody service applications, Bitcoin investment products, and public endorsements.

At the same time, Bitcoin briefly spiked to $67,000 as geopolitical tensions around Iran escalated. A separate story emerged showing Iranian miners producing Bitcoin at a cost of roughly $1,325 per coin — a ~50x return — despite severe local electricity shortages.

## Why It Matters

Bank sentiment is a lagging indicator, but it's a meaningful one. It signals that Bitcoin is completing its journey from "fringe asset" to "standard allocation."

The last wave of institutional adoption was ETF-driven — asset managers buying Bitcoin on behalf of clients. This is different. Banks represent the infrastructure layer of credit, custody, and settlement. When they integrate Bitcoin into their core services, it changes the asset's liquidity profile and accessibility at a structural level.

## My Take

These banks weren't convinced by the technology. They were convinced by the market cap. When an asset is large enough to ignore, ignoring it becomes a business risk — and banks don't like business risks they're not getting paid for.

Here's the irony: Bitcoin was partly designed to circumvent banks. Now banks want a cut. This is either a sign that Bitcoin won, or that it's being absorbed. History suggests financial systems are better at co-opting challengers than being disrupted by them.

## What to Watch

- Will bank custody services compress Bitcoin volatility?
- How will competition among custodians affect security standards for holders?
- Can Bitcoin's anti-establishment narrative survive full institutionalization?
